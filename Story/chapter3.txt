Chapter 3 — One Minute Left

With seconds to spare, Elior secured the last screw. The watch clicked softly, and the hourglass inside froze, then began to drain normally. The stranger smiled faintly, as if relieved of a great weight.
“Thank you,” he said, slipping the watch into his coat. “You won’t remember me after this.”
Before Elior could ask why, the stranger turned the winding key—and the world blurred.
When Elior blinked, he was standing at his shop counter again, morning sunlight spilling through the windows. The bell above the door jingled. A tall man stepped in, holding a silver pocket watch… its hands frozen at 11:59.
