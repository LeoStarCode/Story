Chapter 1 — The Stranger at Closing Time

Elior ran a small clock shop on the corner of Ash Lane, a place where the air always smelled faintly of oil and brass. He had fixed everything from pocket watches to ancient tower bells, but business was slow in recent years.
One rainy evening, just as he was turning the “Closed” sign, the bell above the door jingled. A tall man in a long, dark coat stepped in, dripping rainwater on the wooden floor. In his gloved hand, he held a silver pocket watch, its glass cracked and its hands frozen at 11:59.
“I need this repaired,” the stranger said, his voice unusually calm. “Before midnight.”
