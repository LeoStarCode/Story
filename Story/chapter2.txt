Chapter 2 — The Hourglass Inside

Elior worked fast, his tools clinking under the dim glow of the desk lamp. But as he pried open the watch’s back panel, he froze—inside, instead of gears, sat a tiny hourglass, the sand trickling upward instead of down.
“That’s… impossible,” Elior whispered.
The stranger, now standing uncomfortably close, replied: “It’s not a watch. It’s a promise I must keep. When the last grain reaches the top, time itself will rewind for me—but only if it’s fixed.”
The sand was almost done rising. Elior’s hands trembled. This wasn’t just about repairing a watch—this was about repairing time.
